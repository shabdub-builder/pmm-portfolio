Operating Model

AMPED Teams.

AMPED = AI · Marketing · Product · Engineering · Design — a cross-functional squad of decision makers who meet in a 30-minute weekly standup to drive a launch as a shared goal. I first built this model at Square, where a 44-person EPD org owning squareup.com hadn't shipped a new web experience in over six months. The diagnosis: no decision-making process, and no empathy for what cross-functional teams actually do. The first AMPED squad shipped a homepage overhaul in weeks instead of months — coordination, not more work.

At Honeycomb, the model evolved: the "A" went from Analytics to AI. Claude Cowork joins each squad as a teammate for competitive research and message testing — instead of a PMM building messaging alone, the whole squad prompts and shapes it together. Each squad pairs a PMM and growth marketer with a PM, an EM and engineers, and a UI/UX designer.

Cadence
30-minute weekly standup, moderated by me. Only the moderator and squad members talk — guests are listen-only. Each function answers four questions: what did we ship this week, what progress did we make towards the campaign, any risks, and what conversations do we need to have outside this meeting.
Reporting
The AMPED squad reports status to cross-functional teams in the bi-weekly GTM interlock meeting — keeping the wider org informed without slowing the squad down.

ICP & Messaging Framework

Push the right product to the right team.

At Datadog I codified a 3-step messaging process used across 10+ products and a 10-person PMM team. The same framework now runs at Honeycomb.

Stage 0 — Write
PMM and PM align on the messaging hypothesis. The output is a Product Messaging Manifesto: who the buyer is, what problem we solve, and why we win.
Stage 1 — Pitch
PMM tests the hypothesis with sellers and prospects. Output: v1 pitch deck, v1 demo script, v1 cross-sell messaging. Real conversations before any design investment.
Stage 2 — Roll Out
PMM interviews customers, develops proof points, finalizes the deck and website copy. Every claim is validated. Update cycle: every 6–12 months.
